A Letter from Our Pastor

Dear friends in Christ,

Summer comes slowly to the mountains, and when it arrives it fills our valley with visitors, hikers, and families home for the season. It is a fitting time to remember that our two parishes have always been a place of welcome — for the miner and the rancher, the newcomer and the lifelong neighbor.

In these pages you’ll find where we’ve been and where we’re going: the state of our giving, the ministries quietly holding us together, and the feasts and events that will mark the months ahead. I am grateful for every one of you.

May this summer be, for your household, a season of rest in the Lord.

— Fr. Francisco Higuera

St. Vincent de Paul

Quietly Meeting the Valley’s Needs

Each month our SVdP conference helps a dozen local families with rent, utilities, and groceries — no questions beyond “how can we help?” This spring they distributed over $4,200 in direct assistance.

“We’re just neighbors helping neighbors. The Lord does the rest.”— Conference volunteer

Altar & Rosary Society

Keeping the Lamp Lit in Roslyn

For generations the women of the Altar & Rosary Society have cared for the sanctuary, the linens, and the flowers — and gathered weekly to pray the Rosary for the needs of the parish and town.

“We pray for every family in Roslyn by name, over the year. It’s our gift to them.”— Society member
