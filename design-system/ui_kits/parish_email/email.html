“Her many sins have been forgiven, for she has shown great love.”

From Fr. Francisco

This Sunday’s Gospel finds Jesus at dinner in the home of a Pharisee, where an uninvited woman weeps at his feet. The room is quick to judge her. Jesus is not. He sees her love, and he names it out loud.

It is easy, in a small town, to know each other’s histories. The Lord asks us to see one another the way he saw that woman: not by the worst day, but by the love that brings us through the church door at all.

Who is the one person this week I find hardest to forgive — and what would it cost me to begin?
